Privacy Notice

How we use your survey responses

JOMO  ·  Last updated: March 2026

This notice explains how your responses to the JOMO market research survey will be used. It is short and plain-English. Please read it before completing the survey.

1. Who we are

This research is conducted by heyjomo Limited — a new service being developed to help people find the right place to meet. heyjomo Limited is a company incorporated in England and Wales (company number 17076218), trading as JOMO.

For the purposes of UK data protection law, heyjomo Limited is the data controller for this survey.

Contact: dan@heyjomo.co

2. What data we collect

The survey collects your answers to four questions about how you currently coordinate meeting places with others. No name, email address, or other identifying information is collected directly by JOMO through the survey itself.

If you are taking part via Prolific, Prolific Academic Ltd handles your participation identity (name, payment details, demographics) separately under their own privacy policy. JOMO receives only your anonymised survey responses.

3. Why we collect it and our lawful basis

PurposeLawful basis (UK GDPR)
Understanding whether coordinating meeting places is a meaningful problem for people in LondonLegitimate interests — conducting market research to develop a new product that aims to solve this problem
Informing product design and feature prioritisation for the JOMO serviceLegitimate interests — using aggregated research insights to build a better product

We have assessed that our legitimate interest in understanding the problem we are trying to solve does not override your interests or rights, particularly because: the data is non-sensitive, participation is voluntary, and responses are collected anonymously.

4. How we use your responses

Your responses will be used to:

  • Validate whether the problem JOMO aims to solve is real and widespread
  • Inform the design and development of the JOMO product
  • Support internal reporting and any investor or partner communications about market demand

Responses are analysed in aggregate. We do not attempt to identify individuals from their responses.

5. Who we share your data with

We do not sell, rent, or share your survey responses with third parties for marketing or commercial purposes.

Responses may be shared in aggregated, anonymised form (e.g., "72% of respondents said...") in the following contexts:

  • Internal product and business planning
  • Communications with potential investors or advisors
  • Published research summaries (if applicable, no individual responses identified)

We use standard tools to store and analyse responses (e.g., Google Sheets or equivalent). These tools may store data on servers outside the UK; where they do, we rely on appropriate safeguards.

6. How long we keep your data

Survey responses will be retained for a maximum of two years from the date of collection, after which they will be deleted or anonymised beyond recovery.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your responses (note: because responses are anonymous, we may not be able to locate your specific responses)
  • Restriction — ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Object — object to our processing on legitimate interest grounds
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at dan@heyjomo.co. We will respond within one calendar month.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK supervisory authority, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Phone: 0303 123 1113

8. Changes to this notice

If we make material changes to how we use your data, we will update this notice and revise the date above. For active survey participants, we will seek fresh consent if required.
